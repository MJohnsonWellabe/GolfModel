# Bite-Sized Golf
## Creative Reference Guide
Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the creative inspiration behind Johnson's Golf.

These references are **not** intended to be copied directly.

Instead, they identify the qualities that make great golf games memorable.

Whenever implementation decisions are unclear, Claude should reference this document before making design choices.

Johnson's Golf should feel inspired by these games while developing its own unique identity.

---

# Primary Creative Goal

The player should immediately think:

> "This feels like a real golf game."

NOT

> "This feels like Mario Golf."

The experience should be polished, strategic, satisfying, and visually beautiful.

The game should reward thoughtful decisions while remaining accessible to players of every age.

---

# Primary Inspirations

## Everybody's Golf (Hot Shots Golf)

### What We Love

• Immediate accessibility

• Easy controls

• Difficult mastery

• Beautiful environments

• Satisfying swing rhythm

• Excellent pacing

• Strong camera work

• Charming presentation

• Courses that encourage strategic decisions

### Avoid

Overly exaggerated characters

Extreme cartoon proportions

Arcade physics that ignore golf strategy

---

## EA Sports Tiger Woods PGA Tour (2004–2008)

### What We Love

Presentation

Broadcast-style cameras

Clean menus

Course atmosphere

Shot anticipation

Ball flight

Replay cameras

Professional polish

Club selection interface

Excellent pacing

### Avoid

Simulation complexity

Overwhelming menus

Too many statistics during gameplay

---

## Golf Story

### What We Love

Clean UI

Readable environments

Excellent pacing

Fast gameplay

### Avoid

Flat environments

Minimal camera movement

## PGA Tour 2K

### What We Love

Reference for:

# Art Style References

Johnson's Golf should aim for:

70% believable realism

30% stylization

Never fully cartoon.

Never full simulation.

Examples:

✔ Rich grass textures

✔ Soft shadows

✔ Bright colors

✔ Slight exaggeration

✔ Readable hazards

✔ Premium lighting

---

# Camera References

Every shot should feel cinematic.

Reference:

Tiger Woods PGA Tour

The camera should:

Build anticipation.

Celebrate good shots.

Show ball flight clearly.

Highlight great approaches.

Never become distracting.

The player should enjoy simply watching the golf ball travel.

# Animation References

Golf swings should resemble athletic motion.

Reference:

Professional golf broadcasts

Weight transfer

Hip rotation

Balanced finish

Relaxed follow-through

---

# User Interface References

Reference:

Apple design philosophy

Large touch targets

Minimal clutter

High readability

Smooth animation

Every button should communicate confidence.

---

# Audio References

Reference:

EA Sports golf games

Soft ambience

Natural birds

Wind

Water

Distinct club sounds

Rewarding hole-out sounds

Subtle celebration music

---

# Progression References

Reference:

Rocket League

Unlock cosmetics.

Not power.

Celebrate improvement.

Reward participation.

Avoid grinding.

Every unlock should feel meaningful.

---

# Economy References

Reference:

Modern mobile games without pay-to-win systems.

Goals:

Simple.

Transparent.

Fair.

Players should always know:

How they earned coins.

Why they earned coins.

What they can buy next.

---

# Character Design References

Golfers should look like real people with personality.

Different heights.

Different builds.

Different swings.

Different celebrations.

Different idle animations.

Players should immediately recognize their favorite golfer.

---

# Technical Inspiration

Reference:

Nintendo polish philosophy.

Small details matter.

Examples:

Tiny camera movements.

Grass animation.

Shadow softness.

Sound layering.

Menu transitions.

Consistent frame rate.

The player notices polish even if they cannot explain why.

---

# Things Johnson's Golf Must Never Become

Mario Golf.

A golf simulator.

A menu-heavy RPG.

Pay-to-win.

An animation showcase.

A graphics benchmark.

A physics sandbox.

Every feature should support golf first.

---

# The "Premium Test"

Whenever a new feature is complete, ask:

Would this feel out of place in a professionally published console golf game?

If yes,

redesign it.

If no,

continue polishing.

---

# The "One More Round Test"

The game succeeds if players consistently think:

"I can play one more quick round."

Every design decision should encourage that feeling.

---

# The "Smile Test"

The first drive.

The first birdie.

The first long putt.

The first chip-in.

The first eagle.

The first tournament win.

The first cosmetic unlock.

Each should create a genuine emotional reaction.

Johnson's Golf is ultimately about creating memorable moments.

If a feature increases memorable moments, it belongs.

If it does not, it should be reconsidered.

---

# Final Creative Statement

Johnson's Golf should not try to be the most realistic golf game.

It should strive to be the most enjoyable golf game.

Players should remember:

The beautiful courses.

The satisfying shots.

The exciting putts.

The rewarding progression.

The polished presentation.

The friendly competition.

When someone closes the game, they should immediately want to play another round.

That is the standard every design decision should support.
