# Course Design Bible

**Status:** AUTHORITATIVE (living identity register) — approved 2026-07-20.
Display names, restoration intent, and the Port Johnson direction reflect
owner decisions recorded in `04_IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` §6.
**Scope:** WHO each course is. The craft of designing any individual hole
(risk/reward, three strategies, fairness, green/bunker/tree rules and the
authoring knobs) stays in `docs/10_COURSE_DESIGN_BIBLE.md`. Ambient-life and
audio detail per course stays in `docs/content/COURSE_ATMOSPHERE_BIBLE.md`.
Global review rules live in `03_GLOBAL_DESIGN_SKILL.md`; environment/boundary
rules live in `01_ENVIRONMENT_ROADMAP.md`.

Every course must have:

1. **A Visual Theme** — recognizable from one screenshot.
2. **A Playing Theme** — a principal source of difficulty that shapes every
   hole's decisions.

## The identity template

Each course entry defines ten fields. When any field is empty or contradicts
another course, the course is not done.

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Visual theme | The one-screenshot identity |
| Playing theme | The strategic identity |
| Terrain language | Landform vocabulary (and what the world's edge looks like) |
| Bunker language | Sand shapes, depth, placement grammar |
| Green language | Size, contouring, surrounds |
| Vegetation language | Species, density, where the corridor is lush vs bare |
| Weather & atmosphere | Light, haze, wind assumption, ambient life |
| Principal difficulty | The ONE thing that makes this course hard |
| Signature visual moments | What players screenshot |
| Prohibited elements | What must never appear on this course |

## Sable Bay (`sablebay`) — production — "the postcard island links"

- **Visual theme:** premier coastal golf (Pebble Beach / Pinehurst №2 energy):
  turquoise sea horizon, an island-green silhouette against flat blue,
  sailboats.
- **Playing theme:** ocean wind, forced carries, precision approaches.
- **Terrain language:** low coastal terraces stepping down to the water. The
  sea IS the world's edge — never cliffs inland, never a void ring.
- **Bunker language:** clean sculpted greenside sand; beach transitions where
  sand meets water.
- **Green language:** modest, firm greens with a safe bail side away from the
  water and a dangerous short-side toward it.
- **Vegetation language:** deliberately sparse — wind-shaped pines with palm
  accents at the shoreline, scattered shore stones. The turf and sea carry the
  look.
- **Weather & atmosphere:** high sun, minimal haze; gulls over the sea line.
- **Principal difficulty:** choosing how much water to challenge in wind.
- **Signature visual moments:** the island/peninsula green; a drive framed
  against open ocean.
- **Prohibited:** inland cliff walls, dense woods, desert rock, autumn color.

## Wildwood Glen (`wildwood`) — production — "the flowering parkland"

- **Visual theme:** championship parkland in bloom — broadleaf woods, azalea/
  cherry blossom, garden beds, distant peaks.
- **Playing theme:** tree-framed doglegs; position golf into manicured greens.
- **Terrain language:** gentle valley parkland; the woods themselves close the
  world — the treeline is the horizon.
- **Bunker language:** formal white-sand shapes cut into turf; greenside
  clusters guarding preferred angles.
- **Green language:** larger, contoured greens with mow-line framing; gardens
  behind greens as backdrops.
- **Vegetation language:** the lushest course — dense broadleaf corridors
  (oak/maple/birch/aspen), flower gardens at decision corners; corridor edges
  must FEEL wooded (recovery from trees is part of the course).
- **Weather & atmosphere:** soft mid-morning light, gentle haze, butterflies
  and songbirds.
- **Principal difficulty:** angles — trees block the lazy line; blocked
  recoveries punish greed.
- **Signature visual moments:** blossom trees + rainbow garden beds behind a
  green; a dogleg framed entirely by woods.
- **Prohibited:** deserts, ocean, exposed rock faces, treeless vistas.

## Timberline (`timberline`) — production — "the high alpine"

- **Visual theme:** pine forests, mountains, granite; thin bright air, mist
  wisps on the treetops.
- **Playing theme:** trees define strategy — every shot is a corridor choice.
- **Terrain language:** mountain benches and rises; granite outcrops as
  accents; the conifer wall closes the world (no backdrop ridge needed).
- **Bunker language:** fewer, deeper bunkers at corridor pinch points.
- **Green language:** cool-toned, smaller greens; elevation change into
  greens matters (uphill/downhill club choice).
- **Vegetation language:** conifer walls (spruce/pine, birch accents), ferns/
  stumps/logs as forest-floor scatter. Full density at the corridor edge —
  playing "out of the trees" is the course.
- **Weather & atmosphere:** crisp alpine light, low haze, cool tint; hawks
  circling; the quietest course.
- **Principal difficulty:** the woods — commit to a line or pay a recovery.
- **Signature visual moments:** a lone specimen pine at a carry corner; still
  water reflecting sky and conifers.
- **Prohibited:** palms, ocean, red rock, formal gardens.

## Port Johnson Links (`portjohnson`) — production — "the Scottish harbor links"

*(Owner decision 2026-07-20: full Scottish turn. The re-theme ships only
after the course-variant mechanism exists — see plan §6, decisions 5–6.)*

- **Visual theme:** raw Scottish links weather over a working harbor —
  overcast pewter sky, drizzle-washed light, heather and fescue over
  brown-gold firm turf; moored boats and stone walls in the haze.
- **Playing theme:** wind (20–30 mph always) and weather — ground game, long
  putts, difficult recoveries.
- **Terrain language:** rumpled low linksland; dunes and firm rolls; the
  harbor, mist and overcast close the world.
- **Bunker language:** revetted pot bunkers and marram-edged sand — the
  classic "don't go in there" hazard.
- **Green language:** big, rolling, firm greens that accept run-ups; long
  lag putts are a core skill here.
- **Vegetation language:** heather-dominant rough with tall marram/fescue,
  almost no trees (a couple of wind-bent broadleaf); character comes from
  turf, sky and sand.
- **Weather & atmosphere:** cool overcast light, drizzle treatment, heavy
  haze; gulls, rope-creak, distant foghorn — the one course where the
  weather itself is scenery.
- **Principal difficulty:** wind — the same hole plays three ways on three
  days.
- **Signature visual moments:** a drive into the mist-grey harbor skyline; a
  revetted pot bunker swallowing a bold line; heather glowing under pewter
  sky.
- **Prohibited:** lush parkland green tones, dense woods, mountains, desert
  rock, bright postcard sunshine.

## Red Hollow (`redhollow`) — development — "the red desert canyon"

- **Visual theme:** sandstone mesas and canyons — emerald fairway ribbons
  through bare red ground, layered red-rock ranges on the horizon.
- **Playing theme:** rock hazards, firm bounces, elevated greens; the canyon
  says "not over there."
- **Terrain language:** the ONE course where cliffs are the native edge:
  mesas, shelves, drop-offs, dry washes. Cliff drops must read as canyon
  geography players route around — never as an arbitrary map edge.
- **Bunker language:** waste sand spilling into play, erosion-bowl pots, red
  sand tones.
- **Green language:** elevated and tiered greens (plateau pads, two-tier
  Devil's Kitchen); long is dead.
- **Vegetation language:** bare red rough by design — rocks ARE the
  vegetation: fair-side boulder fields, wash-lining stones, crest formations.
  No grass rough, no woods. Restoration scope (owner decision): sparse dead
  scrub / dry grasses along the washes and rock clusters only.
- **Weather & atmosphere:** hard midday desert light, warm dust haze,
  vultures on thermals. No water anywhere.
- **Principal difficulty:** rocks and elevation — caroms, forced carries over
  canyon, tee-over-green drops.
- **Signature visual moments:** Rimrock's cliff wall looming over the
  fairway; a drive across the dry Wolf Wash; the tiered green under a mesa.
- **Prohibited:** water, lush rough, broadleaf/conifer woods, gardens.

## Wild Prairie (`wildvalley`) — development — "the sand hills"

- **Visual theme:** bright rolling golden fescue, treeless open sand hills,
  deep blowout bunkers (Sand Valley / Wild Horse / Sand Hills NE).
- **Playing theme:** angles, rolling lies, strategic waste areas.
- **Terrain language:** dune amplitude everywhere; blowouts torn out of
  hillsides; the fescue sea + haze close the world (no walls, no cliffs).
- **Bunker language:** blowouts and waste — lobed, deep, fescue-lipped,
  pulled tight against the turf; the split-fairway bunker asks the tee-shot
  question.
- **Green language:** contour spines aligned to the preferred angle —
  the green rewards the brave line with a puttable look.
- **Vegetation language:** ONE grass everywhere (`heather_fescue_b`) at
  maximum density — the treeless identity is deliberate; density and light do
  the work. No trees, no bushes.
- **Weather & atmosphere:** open midwestern light, gentle haze, butterflies
  and meadow birds over the fescue.
- **Principal difficulty:** angles — position beats power; the wrong side of
  a blowout leaves no shot at the pin.
- **Signature visual moments:** the Kettle's enclosed punchbowl green; a
  fairway splitting around a blowout; wind-brushed gold to the horizon.
- **Prohibited:** trees, water, rock faces, manicured parkland tones.

## Expansion rules

- Expand this document as new courses are added; one section per course.
- No two courses may share a principal difficulty AND a terrain language.
- If a change to a course contradicts its section here, either the change or
  this document is wrong — resolve before merging.
