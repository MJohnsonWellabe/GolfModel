# Content and Live Ops Strategy

**Status:** ACTIVE PLAN

## Objective

Keep Bite-Sized Golf fresh through authored content, rotation, atmosphere, and expression rather than constant permanent-system expansion.

## Content hierarchy

### Evergreen core

- current courses and holes
- swing and putting systems
- characters and Pals
- records and mastery
- achievements
- Store and progression

### Rotating content

- Daily Challenge
- Weekly Featured Round
- featured course or mode
- Store rotation
- seasonal cosmetics
- limited presentation themes

### Expansion content

- new courses
- alternate tee and pin layouts
- additional mastery challenges
- new characters and Pals
- new cosmetic collections

## Course-content standard

Every course requires:

- gameplay identity
- visual identity
- environmental audio identity
- atmospheric movement
- landmark language
- performance budget
- mobile readability review
- authored mastery opportunities
- Daily and Weekly compatibility

New courses should add distinct golf decisions, not merely new scenery.

## Character and Pal standard

Characters and Pals are cosmetic expression.

Characters should have:

- readable silhouette
- personality brief
- stance and idle behavior
- emotional reaction set
- Store presentation
- celebration identity

Pals should have:

- clear visual identity
- unobtrusive in-round behavior
- no gameplay bonuses
- safe placement and animation rules

Current economy policy:

- purchasable characters: 500, 750, or 1,000 coins based on authored rarity and desirability
- purchasable Pals: 500 coins
- starter or progression-granted content remains free where intentionally designed

## Daily Challenge

The Daily should be easy to discover, fast to enter, and meaningfully different without requiring a new rules explanation every day.

Daily configuration may include:

- course or hole set
- fixed or bounded wind
- mode
- scoring target
- authored objective
- reward within approved limits
- presentation copy

Claims must be once per eligible date and resilient to refresh or temporary connectivity loss.

## Weekly Featured Round

The Weekly should create asynchronous shared competition through standardized conditions.

Requirements:

- shared seed or defined conditions
- improvement-only leaderboard submissions
- clear reset schedule
- guest-safe participation where possible
- signed-in persistence and ranking where required
- honest trust limitations until server-authoritative validation exists

## Seasonal content

Seasonal content should primarily use:

- Store and reward cosmetics
- marketing presentation
- course atmosphere variants
- featured playlists
- Daily and Weekly themes
- character presentation

Avoid building one-off mechanics that are difficult to remove or maintain.

## Content calendar

Maintain a rolling calendar with:

- theme
- start and end dates
- featured content
- reward plan
- required assets
- configuration owner
- QA date
- publish date
- rollback plan

## Publishing workflow

1. Draft
2. Validate schema and assets
3. Preview in development
4. Review rewards and economy
5. Schedule or publish to development
6. Manual QA
7. Promote to production
8. Monitor analytics and errors
9. Roll back if required
10. Record outcome

## Reward governance

Admin tools may select rewards only within code-defined limits. High-risk formulas and anti-exploit constraints remain code governed.

Every reward change should consider:

- average earning rate
- item prices
- time-to-goal
- existing balances
- duplicate claims
- guest-to-account merge behavior

## Content success metrics

- entry rate
- completion rate
- improvement attempts
- next-round conversion
- repeat participation
- reward claim success
- Store engagement when content is featured
- performance and error rate

## Anti-clutter rule

The landing experience should surface one or two timely opportunities, not every active system. Live content is successful when it creates a clear reason to play, not when it fills the screen.

## Long-term target

A normal week of content should be configurable, previewed, validated, and published without changing game code.
