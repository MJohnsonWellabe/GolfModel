# Bite-Sized Golf — Executive Vision

**Status:** AUTHORITATIVE  
**Applies to:** All product, design, engineering, content, and administrative work

## The product

Bite-Sized Golf is a polished, browser-first arcade golf game built around short three-hole rounds that are easy to start, satisfying to master, and naturally invite another round.

The game should not compete by being the largest golf simulation. It should win by being the most approachable, beautiful, responsive, and replayable small-format golf experience available in a browser.

## The player promise

> In a few seconds, the player can begin a beautiful three-hole golf round. In a few minutes, the round is complete. The outcome reflects real decisions and execution, and the game gives the player one clear reason to play again.

The experience should feel:

- immediately understandable
- smooth and responsive
- cheerful without feeling childish
- relaxing without becoming passive
- skillful without becoming punishing
- rewarding without manipulation
- premium despite being browser based

## What makes the game distinct

### Three-hole focus

The short format is a product advantage, not a limitation. It enables mobile-friendly sessions, fast rematches, frequent mastery attempts, daily play, and content rotation without requiring a large time commitment.

### Arcade clarity with meaningful skill

Players should understand what happened and why. Aim, club, power, accuracy, wind, lie, elevation, landing area, and putting reads should matter. The game may simplify golf, but it must not become automatic.

### High-quality presentation

The game should look and sound intentional. Camera movement, character reactions, environments, transitions, audio, and result presentation must work together to create perceived quality.

### Respect for the player

There are no pay-to-win mechanics, energy timers, loot boxes, forced ads, or deliberately confusing currencies. Retention comes from mastery, expression, progress, variety, and delight.

### Browser-first accessibility

The game must run well on realistic phones and desktop browsers without installation. Performance, fast startup, clear touch controls, and reliable persistence are core product features.

## Primary audience

Bite-Sized Golf is designed for:

- people who enjoy arcade golf but do not want a long simulation session
- mobile players seeking a satisfying game in a short break
- casual golfers who appreciate understandable golf decisions
- competitive players who enjoy improving scores and mastering holes
- collectors who value characters, Pals, cosmetics, and seasonal identity

The game should welcome a first-time player while retaining enough depth for repeated play.

## The long-term vision

Bite-Sized Golf should become a durable small game that can stay fresh through authored content rather than permanent mechanical expansion.

The long-term operating model is:

1. Maintain a highly polished, stable core game.
2. Add atmospheric and character quality.
3. Improve tools so new content can be authored without code changes.
4. Rotate Daily, Weekly, seasonal, and featured experiences.
5. Expand courses, layouts, characters, Pals, and cosmetics selectively.
6. Use real player behavior to guide tuning.

## Strategic priorities

In order:

1. Gameplay responsiveness and correctness
2. Production stability
3. Mobile usability
4. Visual and audio polish
5. Replayability and mastery
6. Content authoring efficiency
7. Sustainable Live Ops
8. Selective content expansion

A lower priority must never damage a higher one.

## What Version 2.0 means

Version 2.0 is not defined by a large feature count. It is the transition from a successfully built game into a mature, maintainable product.

V2.0 should establish:

- a permanent protected development environment
- a disciplined release pipeline
- premium camera, animation, audio, and environmental polish
- stronger character personality
- distinctive course atmosphere
- repeatable content and Live Ops processes
- internal authoring tools
- evidence-based tuning
- a clear documentation system for future Claude work

## Success signals

The strongest player reactions are:

- “That felt smooth.”
- “I knew why I missed.”
- “That looked great.”
- “Let me try that hole again.”
- “I have time for one more round.”
- “I want to unlock that character.”
- “This does not feel like a browser game.”

## Executive decision rule

When choosing between more features and better execution, choose better execution unless real player evidence demonstrates a missing capability.

Bite-Sized Golf becomes special through coherence, not accumulation.
