# Bite-Sized Golf — Player Experience

**Status:** AUTHORITATIVE

## Target experience

A player should be able to open the game, understand the next action, begin a round, complete three holes, understand the result, and choose Replay or Play Next without confusion or unnecessary delay.

## Emotional arc of a round

### Arrival

The game feels welcoming and alive. The player sees a clear primary action rather than a dashboard of competing demands.

### Anticipation

Course framing, character presentation, wind, terrain, and target information create a readable decision.

### Execution

Touch or pointer input responds immediately. The swing meter, aim controls, True Vision, camera, and sound communicate state without fighting for attention.

### Flight and result

The camera makes the shot easy to follow. Impacts feel physical. The player can tell whether the outcome came from aim, power, accuracy, wind, lie, elevation, or course geometry.

### Recovery

A poor shot should create a new golf problem, not a feeling that the game cheated. Recovery shots should remain interesting.

### Completion

Results celebrate without delaying the next decision. Score, personal-best comparison, records, mastery, and one contextual objective are visible with progressive disclosure.

### Continuation

Replay restarts the same experience quickly. Play Next names the destination and advances through a stable course rotation. Compatible settings are preserved.

## New-player principles

- Teach by doing.
- Introduce one concept at a time.
- Avoid lengthy tutorials before the first shot.
- Use contextual hints that disappear once understood.
- Keep advanced information available but not constantly exposed.
- Let players complete a satisfying round before asking them to manage progression systems.

## Returning-player principles

Returning players should immediately see one good reason to play:

- continue a streak
- attempt the Daily Challenge
- improve a course record
- finish a mastery star
- enter the Weekly Featured Round
- unlock a nearby cosmetic goal

Do not display every reason simultaneously.

## Failure philosophy

Failure should be legible and recoverable. The player should think, “I can do that better,” rather than, “I do not know what happened.”

Avoid:

- unexplained penalties
- hidden assists changing outcomes
- camera angles that obscure misses
- excessive celebration after routine results
- immediate monetization prompts after failure

## Delight moments

Delight should be concentrated around meaningful events:

- perfect timing
- dramatic approaches
- long putts
- hole-outs
- personal bests
- mastery completion
- achievement unlocks
- character-specific reactions
- atmospheric course moments

Celebrations should scale with importance. Routine actions need polish, not spectacle.

## Session shape

A healthy session may be one round or several. The game should not shame a player for leaving after three holes. The best retention mechanism is a satisfying ending paired with a compelling next option.

## Accessibility expectations

- Important state is not communicated by sound alone.
- Important state is not communicated by color alone.
- Touch targets are forgiving.
- Text is readable on a 360×800 viewport.
- Reduced-motion behavior is supported for major camera and celebration effects.
- Settings apply immediately and persist reliably.

## Player trust

The game should be honest about rewards, prices, challenge requirements, and leaderboard limitations. Guest players should retain a coherent experience. Sign-in should add persistence and social value rather than gate the core game.

The desired outcome is a game that feels easy to enter, deep enough to improve at, and pleasant enough to revisit.
