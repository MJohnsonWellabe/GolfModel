# Bite-Sized Golf — Product Philosophy

**Status:** AUTHORITATIVE

## Product thesis

Bite-Sized Golf succeeds by making a complete round feel effortless to begin and rewarding to repeat. It should occupy the space between a toy-like tap game and a demanding golf simulator.

## Core loop

1. Enter a round quickly.
2. Read a clear golf situation.
3. Make a meaningful decision.
4. Execute a satisfying shot.
5. Understand the result.
6. Complete three holes.
7. See progress, records, or a next objective.
8. Replay or Play Next without friction.

## Healthy retention model

Retention should come from:

- improving personal records
- mastering authored hole challenges
- Daily and Weekly variety
- collecting characters, Pals, and cosmetics
- seeing expressive celebrations
- competing asynchronously
- learning courses more deeply
- enjoying a beautiful five-minute break

Retention should not come from:

- energy limits
- loot boxes
- pay-to-win
- forced advertisements
- excessive notifications
- currency confusion
- punishment for missing a day
- intentionally slow navigation

## Product boundaries

Bite-Sized Golf is not trying to become:

- a full eighteen-hole simulation platform
- a golf management game
- a social network with golf attached
- a casino economy
- a menu-heavy collection dashboard
- a visual benchmark that performs poorly on normal phones

## Progression philosophy

Progression should make play feel acknowledged, not make the base game feel incomplete.

Good progression rewards:

- play
- mastery
- improvement
- consistency
- exploration
- participation in featured content

Progression should remain understandable with a small number of surfaces and currencies.

## Economy philosophy

Coins represent earned cosmetic purchasing power. Prices should create goals without creating unreasonable grind. The current intended character range is 500–1,000 coins by rarity and Pals cost 500 coins unless explicitly free or granted through progression.

Economy changes require an earning-rate review. Never inflate both prices and rewards merely to create larger numbers.

## Content philosophy

New content should reuse and deepen the core systems. A new hole, alternate pin, challenge rule, atmospheric layer, character reaction, or tournament can add freshness without increasing interface complexity.

## Decision framework

When considering an idea, rank it on:

- player value
- fit with the three-hole promise
- mobile clarity
- performance risk
- replay value
- authoring cost
- maintenance cost
- ability to configure through tools

High-value, low-complexity, reusable improvements should be favored.

## Quality bar

A feature is not complete when it merely functions. It is complete when it:

- feels coherent with the rest of the game
- works on mobile
- respects settings
- degrades safely
- is measurable
- is tested
- is documented
- does not leak resources
- has polished entry and exit states

The product should feel deliberately small, not unfinished.
