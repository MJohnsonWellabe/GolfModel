# 01_PROJECT_VISION.md

## Bite-Sized Golf

### Project Vision Document

**Version 1.0**

---

# Mission Statement

Johnson's Golf is a mobile-first golf game designed to capture everything that makes golf rewarding while removing everything that makes golf intimidating.

The game should be immediately understandable to someone who has never played golf, while remaining deep enough that experienced players continually improve through skill, strategy, and thoughtful decision making.

The design goal is to create a game that players continue returning to years after release because every round feels slightly different and every shot matters.

This is not intended to be a simulation.

It is also not intended to be a casual arcade game.

Instead, Johnson's Golf lives in the middle of those two extremes.

The game should combine:

* the accessibility of Hot Shots Golf / Everybody's Golf
* the presentation of classic EA Sports Tiger Woods
* the strategic decision making of real golf
* the convenience of modern mobile gaming

The player should always feel that success comes from making good decisions rather than simply reacting quickly.

---

# Core Philosophy

Every feature added to Johnson's Golf should reinforce one or more of these principles.

## Easy to Learn

A new player should understand the controls within one minute.

No lengthy tutorials should be required.

The interface should always prioritize clarity.

---

## Difficult to Master

Although the controls are simple, scoring well should require practice.

Good players should consistently outperform average players.

There should always be room for improvement.

---

## Reward Intelligence

Players should think before every shot.

Club selection matters.

Wind matters.

Shot shape matters.

Spin matters.

Landing zones matter.

Green reading matters.

If players ignore these systems they should score worse.

---

## Never Help the Player Secretly

Johnson's Golf should never manipulate gameplay behind the scenes to make players feel successful.

Perfect swings should only happen because the player executed them.

Good shots should feel earned.

Bad shots should feel fair.

The player should trust the game.

---

## Mobile First

Everything should feel designed specifically for touch controls.

Menus should require very few taps.

Rounds should comfortably fit into five minutes.

Players should be able to enjoy one quick round or several hours of play.

---

# What Johnson's Golf Is

Johnson's Golf is:

* strategic
* satisfying
* polished
* replayable
* competitive
* rewarding
* skill-based
* family friendly
* welcoming
* visually beautiful

---

# What Johnson's Golf Is Not

Johnson's Golf is not:

* Mario Golf
* a physics sandbox
* a hyper-realistic simulator
* pay-to-win
* dependent on luck
* full of hidden gameplay assistance
* overloaded with unnecessary menus

Whenever uncertainty exists, development should move toward realism in gameplay and polish in presentation while maintaining accessibility.

---

# Visual Identity

The world should look vibrant without becoming cartoonish.

Courses should feel like places people would genuinely want to play.

Visual priorities:

* realistic grass
* beautiful lighting
* believable trees
* textured terrain
* detailed bunkers
* dynamic water
* soft shadows
* smooth camera movement
* modern interface
* premium presentation

The visual target is closer to modern Everybody's Golf than Mario Golf.

---

# Gameplay Identity

Every shot should ask the player a question.

Examples:

Can I carry this bunker?

Should I hit a lower shot into the wind?

Do I attack the pin?

Do I play safely?

Can I spin this back?

Should I use extra club?

If a shot requires no thinking, the design should be reconsidered.

---

# Emotional Goals

The player should feel:

Excited on the first tee.

Confident after a perfect drive.

Nervous over a difficult putt.

Satisfied after reading a green correctly.

Proud after earning a birdie.

Thrilled after making an eagle.

Motivated to play one more round.

The game should create memorable moments rather than repetitive ones.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Johnson's Golf is intended to become a complete golf platform rather than a single release.

The architecture should support future additions without requiring major redesign.

Planned long-term growth includes:

* additional championship courses
* additional fantasy courses
* more golfers
* career mode
* seasonal tournaments
* daily challenges
* cosmetics
* progression
* achievements
* player accounts
* asynchronous online tournaments
* community events
* statistics
* additional game modes

Every new system should fit naturally within the existing design philosophy.

---

# Definition of Success

A player who downloads Johnson's Golf should think:

> "This feels like a real golf game."

After ten rounds they should think:

> "I'm getting better."

After fifty rounds they should think:

> "I still have things to improve."

After one hundred rounds they should still be unlocking goals, improving scores, and wanting to play one more round.

That is the experience Johnson's Golf should strive to create.

---

# Development Principles

Every feature should satisfy these questions before implementation:

1. Does this make the game more fun?
2. Does this increase meaningful player choice?
3. Does this improve replayability?
4. Does this improve presentation?
5. Does this preserve simplicity?
6. Does this respect mobile-first design?
7. Does this make the game feel more premium?

If the answer is "no" to most of these questions, the feature should be redesigned or rejected.

---

# Final Vision Statement

Johnson's Golf should become the definitive mobile three-hole golf game.

Players should recommend it because it is polished, rewarding, strategic, beautiful, and endlessly replayable—not because it imitates another game, but because it delivers an experience that feels uniquely its own.
