# 19 — Executive Design Recommendations (v1.1)

Written at the close of the v1.0 executive polish pass, as Creative Director /
Executive Producer / Live-Service Designer / Mobile Product Lead. Every idea
below REINFORCES an existing system — nothing here is a new pillar, and nothing
should ship if it dilutes the core promise: **a beautiful three-hole round you
can finish on a coffee break, where good decisions beat lucky ones.** The bar for
inclusion is a single question: does it make the player think *"just one more
round"*?

v1.0 already has the hard parts: trustworthy shot physics, True Vision, a swing
meter that rewards touch, Fire Mode, a 50-level Season Pass, a store, daily
challenge, AI + live tournaments, 1v1 / scramble, 25 characters, 13 pals, four
distinct courses, cloud profiles + leaderboards. The gaps are not *features* —
they're **loops, feedback, and reasons to come back tomorrow**.

## Tier 1 — highest "one more round" leverage (build first)

1. **End-of-round "next hook" screen.** Today a round ends and the player is
   dropped back to menus. Add a single card that always offers the next pull:
   personal-best delta ("+2 on your Wildwood best — 1 stroke off #1 on your
   friends board"), the daily's remaining reward, and a one-tap **"Rematch / New
   round"**. This is the cheapest retention win in the game — it converts the
   dead moment after a round into the start of the next one. Reuses existing
   records + season data.

2. **A visible daily streak with a gentle catch-up.** The daily challenge exists;
   make the *streak* the habit. Show a small flame counter on the landing page,
   a 7-day calendar, escalating (not punishing) rewards, and a single "streak
   freeze" token per week so one missed day doesn't nuke a 30-day streak. The
   code already tracks `dailyStreak` — this is surfacing + a token, not new
   systems. (Note: finish unifying the daily date key to one timezone, already
   flagged in the progression work.)

3. **Three-star hole mastery (a collection you play *for*).** Each hole awards
   1–3 stars (par-or-better / birdie / the hole's specific challenge — e.g.
   "hold the island green in regulation"). A course card shows stars/12; a
   profile shows stars/48. This gives the existing four courses enormous replay
   depth with ZERO new content, turns "I already played Wildwood" into "I have 7
   of 12 stars there," and feeds naturally into achievements.

## Tier 2 — depth, delight, and expression (strong follow-ups)

4. **Achievements with teeth, surfaced at the moment they happen.** A structured
   achievement set (first eagle, chip-in, 3-in-a-row Fire, sub-par on every
   course, a hole-in-one) with a satisfying in-round toast + a profile shelf.
   The store/season already model "owned items"; achievements reuse that shape.
   Tie a few to cosmetic unlocks so they feed player expression, not just a list.

5. **Surprise moments — earned, never random-feeling.** Reinforce Fire Mode and
   True Vision with authored payoffs: a special camera + crowd reaction on a
   hole-in-one or a "saved from the water" recovery; a rare "golden lie" bonus on
   the daily. The rule from the GDD holds — *good decisions beat lucky ones* — so
   surprises reward skill (a clutch putt, a committed Fire streak), never the RNG.

6. **Player expression on the course.** The store sells cosmetics; let them be
   *seen*. A chosen ball trail on every shot, a celebration emote after a great
   hole, a nameplate/frame earned from achievements. Cheap, high-charm, and it
   makes the store purchases feel worth showing off (which sells more of them).

7. **Course "mood" moments.** Each course already has a distinct identity (haze,
   backdrop, palette). Add small time-of-day or weather variants as a cosmetic
   layer (a Sable Bay sunset, a Timberline morning mist) unlocked via the season
   track — visual delight + collection + a reason to replay a course you "know."

## Tier 3 — social & live-service compounding (once the loops are proven)

8. **Asynchronous friend duels.** Live tournaments already use shareable codes;
   add a lightweight async challenge — "beat my Wildwood round," delivered as a
   code/link, results compared on a mini-board. Async fits the 3-minute session
   far better than requiring both players online, and it turns every good round
   into a shareable brag.

9. **Weekly rotating course + featured pin.** A single weekly "featured setup"
   (a course + a hard pin + a wind seed) that everyone plays on the same
   leaderboard, resetting weekly. Manufactures a communal event out of existing
   content, gives lapsed players a reason to return each week, and pairs with the
   marketing manager you now have for promo.

10. **Seasonal event framing for the Season Pass.** The pass is a 50-level track;
    give each season a *theme* (art, a themed course-mood, a headline reward) and
    a countdown. The rollover logic now shipped means seasons can actually turn
    over — lean into it with a "new season starts in 4 days" hook.

## Deliberately NOT recommended (anti-bloat guardrails)

- **No energy/stamina or pay-to-play-more gates** — kills "just one more round."
- **No loot boxes / gacha pulls for gameplay power** — violates *skill beats
  luck* and the store's "earned, not bought" promise.
- **No 18-hole or "real course" mode for v1.1** — it breaks the coffee-break
  session length that IS the product.
- **No new control scheme / meter rework** — the meter is the heart; polish it,
  don't replace it.
- **No chat/UGC before moderation exists** — social should start async and
  brag-shaped, not open text.

## Sequencing

Ship Tier 1 as a single "come back tomorrow" update (end-of-round hook + streak +
hole stars) — it compounds retention with almost no new content. Then Tier 2 for
delight/expression, then Tier 3 for social once the daily loop is proven by the
numbers. Instrument first: the perf/latency instrumentation added in this pass is
the template — add funnel events (round complete, daily claimed, streak length,
D1/D7 return) so v1.1 decisions are measured, not guessed.
